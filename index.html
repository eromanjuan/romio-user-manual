ROMIO User Manual

ROMIO is an all-in-one workspace: a social feed, direct & group chat, collaborative workspaces with a no-code app builder, and personal tools. This guide walks through every feature.

info Overview

What is ROMIO?

ROMIO is an all-in-one social workspace platform. It brings together three things that usually live in separate apps: a social network (a shared feed where you post and connect with people), a messenger (direct and group chat), and a no-code work platform (team workspaces where you build your own apps, dashboards, automations and reports — without writing any code). On top of that it adds personal productivity tools (calendar, checklist, notes, widgets) and deep personalization (themes, avatar frames, custom profiles).

You use it like a social app — share updates, like, comment, @mention, and message people — and like a workplace app — spin up a workspace for a team, class or project, invite members with roles, and build a custom database app (a task tracker, CRM, grade book, inventory, log — anything) that your members fill in together. Everything is real-time: changes appear instantly across devices, and each page has its own web address so links are shareable.

lightbulb In one line: a social platform where you also build and run your own team workspaces, apps and automations — no coding needed.

Who it's for: teams, communities, clubs, small businesses, and students — anyone who wants to post, chat, organize people, and track their work from a single, customizable app.

login Getting started

Create an account

Sign-up is a quick 3-step wizard (with a progress bar). You can also continue with Google or Facebook from any step.

  1. Step 1 — Your details: first name, surname and email.
  2. Step 2 — Username & password: choose a unique username (availability is checked as you type) and a password (a strength meter guides you; confirm it).
  3. Step 3 — Review & agree: confirm your details, accept the Terms of Service, and press Create account. A verification email is sent — verify before using the app.
badge Every account has a username. It's required at sign-up, and social sign-ins are asked to pick one the first time. Your username is your public @handle.

Sign in

  • Log in with your username or email + password. Tick Remember me to stay signed in.
  • Google and Facebook sign-in are available (these require the provider to be enabled in the project).
  • Forgot your password? Use the reset link to receive an email.
verified_user A green Verified badge appears once your email is confirmed. You control whether it shows on your public profile (see Settings → Public profile).

  • Hide — the post becomes visible only to you (unhide anytime).

chat Messages & chat

Open Work → Messages for a two-pane chat (conversation list + live thread; on phones the thread slides in).

Sending messages

  • Text — type and press Enter (Shift+Enter for a new line).
  • Attachments — click the paperclip to send a file. Images show inline (click to open full size); other files appear as a download card.
  • Clickable links — any URL or email you type becomes a safe, tappable link automatically.
  • Encrypted messages — click the lock to turn on encryption, choose a method (AES-256 for real security, or the classic Vigenère / Caesar ciphers), and set a secret key. The recipient enters the same key to read it. The key is never sent or stored — share it another way.

Online status

In a direct chat you can see whether the other person is Online (green dot) or their "Last seen" time. A dot also appears on their avatar in the conversation list, and their status shows on their profile.

Group chats

When a workspace is created, a group chat is made automatically with the owner and all members. As members are added/removed (or the workspace is renamed), the group chat updates. Group chats show the workspace's icon.

Direct messages

Start a 1-to-1 chat three ways: Messages → New then search a person; the Message button in Search results; or Message on someone's profile. DM avatars show the person's current photo, and new messages raise the Messages badge and the notification bell.

lock Encryption uses a shared secret: only AES-256 is cryptographically strong (Caesar/Vigenère are for fun/obfuscation). The plaintext is never stored — only the ciphertext travels with the message.

account_circle Your profile

Open My Profile from the sidebar — or click your name/avatar at the bottom of the sidebar, which is a shortcut to it.

Profile photo & frames

  1. Click your avatar to open the photo viewer. From there you can Upload a new photo, Edit the current one, or Remove it (back to your initials).
  2. In the editor: drag to reposition, zoom (slider or wheel), rotate 90°, and flip. A guide shows the crop, then Save. Your photo updates on your profile, sidebar and posts instantly.
  3. Frames — in the viewer, pick a decorative frame: presets (Gold, Neon, Rainbow, Fire, Ocean…) or a Custom one where you choose a solid color or gradient, angle, and thickness. It shows around your avatar everywhere.

Bio & links

  • Bio — a short about-you; long bios show 3 lines with a "Show more" reveal.
  • Links — add multiple labelled links (portfolio, socials, etc.) in Settings → Profile. They appear as a row on your profile.

View as visitor

Click View as (top-right of My Profile) to preview your profile exactly as other people see it, respecting your visibility settings. Use Back to my profile to return.

dashboard Workspaces

A workspace is a team space with its own members, roles, dashboard and chat.

Create, open & switch

  • With no workspace yet, the Workspace page shows Create workspace. Give it a name, icon/color or an image.
  • In Settings → Select workspace, click Open on any workspace to switch to it (it becomes your current one). Changes appear instantly — no reload.

Members, roles & invites

  • Owners manage members from the workspace gear → Members: add people, set roles, remove, or bulk-change roles.
  • Roles: Owner (full), Editor (read+write), Viewer (read-only), or Custom permissions.
  • Invites and join requests are supported; owners approve/decline requests.

Deleting a workspace

build Creating a no-code app

An app is a custom data tool you build with no code — a task tracker, CRM, bug list, inventory, time log, anything. Each app is a set of records (rows), and you define the fields (columns) that make up a record.

1. Create the app

  1. In a workspace, click + Add app.
  2. Give it a name, and pick an icon and color.
  3. You can start from scratch, or import a template — a .rom.json bundle (several sample apps are provided: project tracker, bug tracker, sprint board, client CRM, time log, feature backlog).

2. Add fields

  1. Open the app and choose Add field.
  2. Pick a field type (see the Field types reference below) and give it a label.
  3. Configure the field's options where relevant:
    • Category / Status / Multi-select — add the choosable options, each with its own color.
    • Calculation — pick the number fields and the operation (sum, difference, product, etc.).
    • Days Between — choose the two date fields to measure between.
    • Auto-number — set a prefix (e.g. TASK-) for IDs like TASK-014.
    • Relationship — choose the target app whose records this field links to.
    • Number / Money / Rating / Progress — set ranges/format as offered.

3. Add & manage records

  • Click + Add (or the app tile's +) to create a record and fill in its fields.
  • Open any record to edit every field, add comments, or delete it. Saving keeps the record open.
  • Use the ‹ / › arrows in the record window to save and jump to the previous/next record without closing — handy for filling in many rows quickly.
  • Records show in a list/table; sort and browse with the built-in paging.

4. Reports & charts

Pin a Report to summarize an app — counts and charts grouped by a field (e.g. tasks by Status). Reports can be added to the dashboard as a Report tile.

5. Automations

Add simple when → then rules: when an item is created, updated, or a field changes to a value (including numeric comparisons like Score ≥ 75), then run an action — Notify or Set a field. Build multi-step actions with Add action. Example: when Completion reaches 100%, set Status to Done.

6. Sharing & installing apps

  • Romio App Market — from an app's Settings, toggle Share this app to publish its structure (fields & automations, never your records) to the market. Anyone can then Add app → Romio App Market and install it into their workspace.
  • Export / import files — download an app as a portable .rom.json bundle, or Add app → Install from a file to bring one in (fields, automations and any included records).
upload_file Apps are portable — share them to the market, or export/import .rom.json bundles between workspaces.

folder Files

Upload and manage your files under Work → Files.

Trash (30-day)

  • Deleting a file moves it to Trash instead of removing it immediately.
  • Open Settings → Trash to see deleted files with a "days left" countdown. Select files to Restore or Delete permanently.
  • Files left in Trash are automatically deleted after 30 days.

quiz FAQ & troubleshooting

"Missing or insufficient permissions"

Usually means you're not signed in with the right account, or you're acting on something you don't own (for example, editing someone else's post, or a workspace you're only a viewer in).

My theme/photo isn't showing on another device

Hard-refresh after signing in. Themes are saved to your account; profile photos and backgrounds are stored inline and load with your profile.

A workspace shows apps I didn't add

Older workspaces may have inherited data before per-workspace isolation. Remove unwanted apps/tiles per workspace, or ask to reset that workspace.

memory Technical notes

  • Built on Firebase (Authentication + Cloud Firestore). Data updates in real time.
  • Firebase Storage is not enabled, so photos, small files and backgrounds are stored inline (compressed) with a size cap; large videos/files are shared by URL/embed instead of upload.
  • Weather uses Open-Meteo; icons use Tabler / Google Material Symbols.
  • Two tabs editing the same workspace builder at once is last-write-wins — edit in one place at a time.
